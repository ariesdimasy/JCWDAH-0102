Computers are electronic devices that process data and perform tasks according to a set of instructions called programs. They have become an essential part of modern life, enabling people to work, communicate, and access information efficiently.

There are various types of computers, ranging from large mainframes used by organizations to personal computers and laptops for individual use. Each type serves different purposes based on processing power, storage capacity, and portability.

Computers operate using both hardware and software components. Hardware refers to the physical parts like the processor, memory, and storage devices, while software includes the operating system and applications that run on the hardware.

With the advancement of technology, computers have become faster, smaller, and more affordable. They play a crucial role in fields such as education, healthcare, business, and entertainment, shaping the way people live and work.

Servicing a computer begins with turning off the device and unplugging it from any power source to ensure safety during maintenance.

Next, carefully open the computer case using the appropriate tools, such as a screwdriver, to access the internal components.

Use compressed air to remove dust and debris from fans, vents, and other internal parts, as dust buildup can cause overheating.

Check all cables and connections to ensure they are secure and not damaged, as loose connections can lead to hardware malfunctions.

Inspect the hard drive and memory modules for signs of wear or failure, and replace them if necessary to maintain optimal performance.

Update the operating system and all installed software to the latest versions to protect the computer from security vulnerabilities.

Run a full antivirus scan to detect and remove any malware or unwanted programs that could affect system stability.

Defragment the hard drive if you are using a traditional HDD, as this can improve data access speeds and overall performance.

Clean the keyboard, mouse, and monitor with appropriate cleaning solutions to maintain hygiene and usability.

Finally, reassemble the computer, plug it back in, and test all functions to ensure the servicing was successful and the system runs smoothly.
